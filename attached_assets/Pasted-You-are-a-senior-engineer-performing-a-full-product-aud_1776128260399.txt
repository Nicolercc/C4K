SECTION 8 — BYTE DESIGN & ANIMATION
══════════════════════════════════

8.1  Is Byte NEVER completely still on any screen?
     At minimum: the idle float (y oscillation) must be 
     running at all times. A static Byte is a dead Byte.

8.2  Does Byte's body sway and float use DIFFERENT 
     timing so they never sync?
     Synchronized motion looks mechanical. 
     Float: 3.5s. Sway: 5s. Never the same duration.

8.3  Do Byte's eyes track the user's mouse cursor?
     Move the cursor slowly across the lesson panel.
     The pupils should follow with a slight lag.
     This is the single most important detail for 
     making kids feel seen by Byte.

8.4  Is the cheer animation a real JUMP (y -22px) 
     with spring physics — not just a scale pulse?
     A scale pulse looks like a notification. 
     A jump looks like joy.

8.5  Does the star burst fire on correct answers?
     8 gold particles should emit from Byte's center 
     and travel outward in different directions.

8.6  Does Byte's sad state include the red flash 
     before drooping?
     The red flash (like a hit in a video game) 
     communicates "ouch" instantly before the droop.

8.7  Do Byte's arms move independently?
     Idle: both arms sway at different speeds.
     Cheer: both arms shoot up simultaneously.
     Think: right arm moves to chin position.
     Sad: both arms droop lower.

8.8  Does the speech bubble use the typewriter effect?
     Characters appear one at a time at 28ms per character.
     Punctuation (. ! ?) pauses 220ms.
     If text still appears all at once: FAIL.

8.9  Does the speech bubble shake when a word 
     is in ALL CAPS?
     "YOU DID IT!" should cause the bubble to 
     micro-shake as those words are typed out.

8.10 Does the speech bubble change background color 
     by Byte's mood?
     idle: white. cheer: light green. think: light orange.
     sad: light red. story: light pink with label.

8.11 Does Byte wave hello when the lesson page first loads?
     Right arm should wave 3 times before settling to idle.

8.12 Does Byte lean forward during story mode?
     The body should tilt -3 degrees toward the screen.
     Byte is sharing something personal — the posture 
     should communicate intimacy.

8.13 Is the story bubble visually distinct from 
     regular speech bubbles?
     It should have a pink left border, pink background, 
     and a small "Byte's story" label at the top.

8.14 Does Byte enter the lesson page from below 
     (y: 60 → 0) rather than just appearing?
     The entrance animation makes Byte feel like 
     they arrived, not like they were always there.

8.15 Is Byte's size appropriate for each screen?
     Onboarding Screen 1: large (180-200px) — hero moment
     Topic picker Screen 2: small (88px) — watching, not dominating
     Lesson panel: medium (96px) — present but not distracting
     Completion screen: large (140px minimum) — celebrating

8.16 Is there a glow or bloom effect behind Byte 
     on the dark background screens?
     A radial gradient glow (rgba(92,62,188,0.3), blur 40px) 
     behind Byte makes them feel luminous rather than pasted on.
